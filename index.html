Keywords

Job Ticket

A job ticket is another word for work order. It's a slip of paper that accompanies the job and gives instructions for printing or recording how much time was spent on the job.

Imposition for printing

A fundaments step in a printing process. It's the arrangment of the product's pages on a printer's sheet. It allows for faster printing and simpflify bindings and minimalizes paper waste.

Work-and-Turn Imposition

A printing plate that has both the front and back of a sheet are put onto the press at the same time, and printed on a double-size sheet of paper. Half the sheet is printing on the top, while the other half is printing on the bottom aka, "back printing".

Sheetwise Imposition

For a double sided printing job, the first side is printed on the "parent" sheet, and then after the ink dries, the printing plates are changed and the paper is turned over to print on the back.

Work-and-Tumble Imposition

One plate contains all the pages to be printed on both sides of the sheet. Once one side is finished printing, the pile of printed sheets is turned over. After it is done printing, the sheets are cut in half which yields two identical units.

Spot Colors vs. Process

Spot Colors

Any color generated by an ink, pure or mixed, that is printed using a single run.

Process Colors

A system of using four standard inks and mixing them on the press to create a wide spectrum of different colors (CMYK).
